About

ETH Montreal is a community-driven initiative to connect our local projects, developers, students, researchers, and artists with the broader international Ethereum community.

We place a strong emphasis on engaging students from our world-class universities and onboard them to the ecosystem. At its core, it is an annual hackathon and conference, but it has further ambitions as a support infrastructure for up-and-coming builders, researchers, and public-goods projects in Montreal.

Mission

There are therefore two facets to our mission:

  1. Off-ramp: bringing value from the ecosystem to Montreal.
    1. inviting leading researchers and builders to speak and connect
    2. organizing virtual and in-person high-quality workshops
    3. providing basic funding in the form of student stipends or small grants to independent developers, researchers, artists whose work align with our principles (provided we succeed in generating excess funds from the hackathon/conference)
  2. On-ramp: onboarding local talents to the Ethereum ecosystem
    1. Engaging technical, social, and artistic talents (particularly from our universities) with the ecosystem. This is achieved by keeping hackathon attendants engaged post the main annual event.
    2. Stimulating academic research into open problems various areas: scalability, coordination, new artistic expressions, and public goods.

Principles

  • Inclusion and diversity: we make conscious and deliberate efforts to increase the diversity of perspectives among ourselves and in the proceedings of our events.
  • Privacy: we do not sell people’s data.
  • Sustainability: we strive to reward all contributors of ETH Montreal, including volunteers
  • Public goods: we have a main focus on promoting projects, builders, artists, and researchers who are working on problems of wide public interest.
  • Consensus: we seek consensus internally and externally with the wider Ethereum community in Montreal.
  • Growth: we strive to grow in both size and impact.
  • Transparency: we avoid conflict of interests as much as possible, and highlight them clearly when unavoidable.
  • Accountability: the roles of each contributor, whether in an governance or execution capacity, is clearly defined, with checks and balances in place to ensure accountability.
  • Transparency: we regularly publish reports detailing our work: how and why decisions were made, how funds were allocated, etc.
